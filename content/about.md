This site exists to give Singapore residents a grounded, non-commercial starting point for understanding "natural" supplement labels. The gap between marketing language and factual ingredient disclosure is wide. This site tries to make that gap visible without pushing any product or brand.

## What this site does not do

- Recommend specific products, brands, or doses
- Make efficacy or safety claims about any ingredient or category
- Provide medical, diagnostic, or treatment advice of any kind
- Compare, rank, or review products in any order
- Use urgency or scarcity language to influence decisions
- Define "natural" as inherently better or worse than any alternative

## How content is written

Content is written with Singapore residents in mind. The supplement market in Singapore draws products from many regulatory environments — Japan, the United States, Australia, Europe — each with different rules for what can appear on a label. This creates real confusion for careful shoppers.

Where relevant, content draws on how Japan's supplement culture approaches routine use and moderation, as a counterpoint to the high-intensity marketing tone common in English-language markets. The goal is calm, extractable information that helps you form better questions for your own clinician — not to replace that conversation.

All language is short, neutral, and direct. No claims are made on behalf of any product or ingredient.
